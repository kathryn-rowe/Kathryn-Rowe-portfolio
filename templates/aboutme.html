About Me
I'm a biologist turned Software Engineer with a tendency towards environmental activism.

I enjoy creating tools that tackle complex, everyday problems. I began coding in 2011 during my Master’s in Geographic Information Technology program where I used Python to automate spatial analysis in order to model stormwater drainage in Boston and spawning habitat for native fish.

Since receiving my Master’s, I’ve used different technologies to help small nonprofits achieve their missions. Whether it's a new web platform, social media, Excel, Adobe Creative Suite, or a CRM, I quickly learn new products and systems that help organizations effectively reach their goals.

I have seen the daily technological barriers organizations face and my goal as a software engineer is to build tools to help them overcome these challenges.
